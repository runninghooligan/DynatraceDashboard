UI Ideas — Active P-Cards + Active INCs with Linked Selection
Standalone mockup showing different layout ideas for two operational tables. Focus areas: compact presentation, handling long labels, clear P-Card ↔ INC relationships, and a selection model that exposes more detail without making the full page too dense.
Idea A: best when you want the cleanest side-by-side comparison. Selection details appear underneath both tables, which keeps the main tables wide enough for real work.

Why it works

Each table stays wide enough to show card number, age, priority, owner, and linked counts without turning every field into a tooltip.

Long label handling

Use a fixed-width table with ellipsis in long columns such as entity, title, and assignment group. Show the full value on hover and again in the inspector.

Best use case

This is the strongest option if operators work both tables continuously and need a balanced P-Card Hub and Incident Hub.

Idea B: best when the linked relationship itself is the centerpiece. A dedicated center rail summarizes direct links, correlated links, confidence, and affected services.
This layout is useful if you want to emphasize correlation, source-versus-symptom analysis, and quick actions like “show only linked rows” or “form swarm.”
Idea C: best when you want the most compact widescreen footprint. The tables remain side by side, but the inspector is shallow and embedded directly under the headers. Good for 34" ultrawide displays.

Why it works

It minimizes vertical travel and avoids a large bottom workspace. Detail is shown in a shallow strip with high-value fields only.

Tradeoff

You get less room for comments and long history. Use a “full details” drawer or modal from this compact selection strip.

Best use case

Use this for wallboard-like daily triage or a fast-moving operations desk where the table itself is the main workspace.
